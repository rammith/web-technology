About Bugatti









Bugatti is a French high-performance luxury automobiles manufacturer founded in 1909 in the then German city of Molsheim, Alsace, by the Italian-born industrial designer Ettore Bugatti.

The company is now owned by the Volkswagen Group and is known for producing some of the fastest and most exclusive cars in the world.

Bugatti cars are renowned for their blend of high-performance, luxury, and design innovation. They are often seen as a symbol of wealth and status.

The company's most famous models include the Bugatti Veyron, Chiron, Divo, La Voiture Noire, and Centodieci.
